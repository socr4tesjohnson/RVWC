Patient guidance

Answers for the most common questions

If you don't see your question here, call or email our team. We're happy to help.

Do I need a referral to be seen?
Many patients are referred by their primary care doctor, surgeon, endocrinologist, or other specialist. Some insurance plans allow self-referral. If you are unsure, call our office and we will help you check your benefits.
What should I bring to my first visit?
Please bring your photo ID, insurance card, a list of medications and allergies, and any recent wound-related records or imaging if available. If you are currently using special dressings or off-loading devices, bring those as well.
How long does wound healing usually take?
Healing time varies based on the type of wound, circulation, infection, blood sugar control, and other health conditions. At your evaluation, we will review your specific situation and outline realistic expectations for healing.
How often will I need to come to clinic?
Many patients are seen weekly or every other week early in treatment, then less often as the wound improves. Your schedule is individualized to your needs and your response to therapy.
Do you coordinate with my other doctors?
Yes. We regularly communicate with your primary care provider, surgeons, vascular specialists, and other members of your care team to ensure your wound care fits within your overall health plan.
What types of treatments do you offer?
Treatments may include debridement, advanced dressings, negative pressure wound therapy, off-loading, compression therapy, and referrals for imaging or vascular evaluation when needed.
If you develop sudden severe pain, spreading redness, fever, foul odor, or concern for a severe infection or loss of blood flow, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency department.
